## What this is

A **workshop-driven** corporate training on Product Management & product mindset, proposed to **SoftServe** through Alex's **Laba** engagement. Goal: help SoftServe's technical & delivery leaders shift from engineering/service thinking to **product thinking**, and drive the org's move from a service model to an **asset/product-oriented** one — building repeatable offerings out of services, accelerators, and reusable assets. (Internally this is part-advisory — it should drive real transformation decisions — but that framing is **never named in client-facing material**.)

**Wall-off note (updated 2026-07-07):** this engagement is **unrelated** to the [SoftServe R&D advisory](../softserve/README.md), and Alex's R&D-derived knowledge of SoftServe stays background-only (never used as content in client-facing material). However, Alex **chose to disclose the advisory role itself**: offer v3's instructor bio includes "Product advisor to SoftServe R&D: repeatable-IP strategy and productization processes" (his Word comment, offer review). So the *fact* of the engagement is now client-visible; the *substance* (strategy materials, internal reads) remains walled off.

## Shape

- **7 modules / 22 sessions** (Alex's strict backbone — no modules/topics added), **2h each**, **online/live** (audience spans UA/PL/BG/US).
- ~**15 participants**: service leaders, capability managers, architects, technical/product owners, delivery & engineering leaders.
- **3–4 groups**, each carrying one real **product** (v3 vocabulary: "product", not "asset", for the group's working item) through the whole lifecycle via cumulative home assignments (**~2–3h each**; classic homework with written feedback OR preparatory work feeding the next session — the facilitated-live-session option was cut per Alex's comment). Working products are selected **by program start** (via pre-work), not in Module 1.
- Session types: **interactive seminar** (theory + instructor's examples + discussion on their cases + review of typical questions / good & bad examples from preceding homework) and **workshop** (hands-on exercise, in groups or all together, ending in a PM artifact OR decisions/action items/ideas). No "demo" label at summary level (M6's live demo lives only inside its module topics — overpromise risk, Alex's comment).
- **M6 practical work = AI-powered PM** (agents/skills/context systems accelerating the participants' own PM workflow — focus areas + first experiments); evals & governance remain seminar/demo content only.
- Final module = **defining next steps** (transformation roadmap + owned commitments).
- **Before start** (offer's Next steps): discovery call (needs/situation/expectations) + information request (current products/services/accelerators/initiatives → feeds workshops, homework, working-asset selection).
