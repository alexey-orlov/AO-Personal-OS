## TL;DR
- Team is building a reusable-assets / expertise model where L3–L4 experts own a domain and mentor a trainee/junior (expert as brain, trainee as hands) to produce assets after project involvement; the cluster team likes the direction.
- Two case-study problems dominate: (1) no single, filterable, centralized repository of case studies across clusters, and (2) case studies are created too late (only at project end), making them stale or never created.
- Agreement to assign content ownership and define a "gated" process (capture at start and end of engagement), plus debate over who is accountable on a project (Engineering Lead favored) and whether presales/free POCs/research count as assets.

## Key points
- **Asset ownership model:** L3–L4 cluster experts own expertise and assets; pair each with a trainee/junior they coach. Suggestion to find an owner now for promising directions (e.g., Oracle accelerators) and train them up.
- **Centralized case-study hub:** Current case studies are dispersed across clusters with different approaches. No way to easily answer "what have we done on AWS / on a given cloud provider." Proposal: a shared, filterable space (e.g., a SharePoint-style table) that sales can filter (cloud provider, etc.) — similar to how sales already use such tables.
- **Content ownership exists partly:** Speaker B already has a content manager (Liudmyla Tatura, replacing Alla who went on maternity leave); not a technical expert but can collect/manage/standardize content. Standardizing this process is in B's yearly goals.
- **Timing of case-study creation:** Creating only at project end fails — at the end success is unclear, many projects are outstaff/client-side and long-running (or never truly "end"). Proposal to capture early (target value / target resolution) for communication to sales, then update at the end. A gate process at start and end of engagement was discussed.
- **Accountability debate:** Cluster Leads won't work; Product Managers don't exist on every project; Project Managers cover only ~20%. Speaker A argues it should be the Engineering Lead on the project; gap acknowledged when no PM/PdM/Eng Lead exists (then only a middle dev is available and can't be strongly required to do it).
- **What counts as an asset:** Minimum one presentation slide, maximum an asset/video. Debate on whether presales demos, free POCs, and research programs (e.g., custom-trained models) qualify. Speaker C argues meaningful presales demos / domain immersion (e.g., insurance AI document-processing pipeline) are expertise worth capturing and reusing; Speaker B cautions only genuinely-done work should count to keep sales' trust.
- **Process idea (Speaker C):** A content manager (non-technical) monitors the presale and active-project pipeline weekly, chases stream owners, and prompts them to fill the table or do a short recorded call; uses the R&D resource planner to see active projects and flag missing case studies.
