## Customer context
- This is an SI-partnership/co-development call, not an end-customer sale. SoftServe wants a streamlined two-way model: (1) push POC customizations back into existing packs, (2) proactively invest in and co-build new packs to drive demand.
- Oracle's pack strategy splits two personas: line-of-business "business outcome" packs (e.g., routing/workforce optimization) and engineer-focused horizontal packs for SIs (e.g., an agent-observability pack bundling LangFuse, MinIO, Traefik).
- Oracle prioritizes by market size, willingness-to-pay, competition, internal capability, short- vs long-term bet, and whether customers/POCs are already lined up. Packs are open source; contributions welcome, ownership line between Oracle-built vs SI-built is intentionally "blurry."
- Stack direction: NVIDIA-prioritized (GPUs + software) but not exclusive; moving from raw IaaS GPUs (OKE) toward platform services — AI Data Platform, dedicated AI clusters, on-demand GenAI service.
- End-customer POCs in flight: **Eriks** (retail; demo delivered today, export-schema rework underway), **Bosch** (workforce/routing optimization; in acceptance), plus **DHL** and **Belron** ("window crack" use case) in pipeline but not yet started.
