## Topics covered
- When to invest in accelerators / repeatable assets vs. internal delivery, given a domain has potential but no clear market demand
- Two models for entering a domain: (1) build deep expertise first and let relevant work organically gravitate in (the "AJK" path), vs. (2) validate demand with the market, then build a solution against validated pipeline
- R&D specificity: niche clusters (Quantum, AGI, Audio, Edge AI, Physics-Informed Neural Networks, Life Sciences) may have little/no current demand, but expert staff can't be re-tasked quickly — idle expertise can occasionally (~10%) produce something
- Sizing investment per niche (e.g., 3 audio engineers vs. an ElevenLabs-scale team) and deciding whether to keep or close a cluster
- Signal for "graduating" an emerging direction (e.g., physics/simulation, NVIDIA-referred clients) from bespoke service to repeatable service/asset — roughly when 3–6 similar projects appear
- Product–cluster pairing (e.g., Leonid + Daria) to identify what can be packaged into assets, since engineers alone often can't do this
- R&D's ability to influence/generate its own pipeline (cited: pipeline changed ~90% in ~1.5 yrs; R&D is the only SoftServe team doing agentic DLC upscaling)
- Need for demoable artifacts (Agentic DLC currently lacks one; old CLI agent reads as outdated to clients)
