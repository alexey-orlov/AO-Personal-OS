## Summary
The conversation argues that the dominant model of shareholder-primacy and short-term investor incentives corrodes company missions and destroys long-term value. Founders can instead structure companies to be 'mission-controlled'—using legal forms, governance design, and selection of aligned trustees/board members—to protect mission and create much longer-lived, higher-value firms. The speaker gives historical and modern case studies (FedMart/Costco, Twilio, Novo Nordisk, Anthropic) plus actionable tools (PBCs, foundations, perpetual purpose trusts) showing how to do this in practice.
