## Summary
Benedict Evans argues AI is a genuine, generational platform shift on the scale of the internet and mobile, but we are still in an early, '1997'-style moment with wide variation in adoption and huge uncertainty about how value will be allocated. He breaks the discussion into capital (who pays for models), deployment (why integration and consultants matter), and change (how jobs and industries will be reshaped), and urges practitioners to accept radical uncertainty and learn by doing. His practical takeaway: the models are powerful but messy; winners may be the companies that wrap them into reliable apps and distribution, not necessarily the model labs themselves.
