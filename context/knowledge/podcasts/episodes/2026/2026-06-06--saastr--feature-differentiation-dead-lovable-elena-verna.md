## Summary
Elena Verna argues that traditional product-feature moats are becoming transient because AI-powered "vibe coding" drastically lowers the cost and time to build features. Instead, durable advantages come from proprietary context — data, network effects, hardware, security/compliance, and brand — plus organizational changes that let teams ship daily and empower builders. She demonstrates how Lovable lets non‑technical builders and small teams create full products quickly and shows real outcomes (e.g., a student-built AI study buddy that reached $130k ARR in 30 days).
