## Summary
Founder Mukun (previously Dunzo) explains how six months of unfettered tinkering with new models after leaving his prior startup produced the insight and prototypes that became Emergent. Instead of building another front-end copilot, the team engineered a full-stack, autonomous multi-agent system and proprietary infra that finishes real, working software. That approach—focus on complete products, aggressive infrastructure work, and 'living on the edge' of model progress—helped Emergent scale to ~8.5M users and an annualized revenue run rate north of $100M within nine months. The throughline: deep, early technical bets on what models will enable, plus obsessing over finish-to-finish product quality, create outsized second-mover advantages.
