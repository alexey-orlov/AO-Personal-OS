## Summary
Benedict Evans analyzes how large language models (LLMs) are reshaping software economics and what that means for SaaS, infrastructure, and industry structure. His throughline: foundation models today look like expensive, fast-evolving infrastructure that may end up commoditized, while the real value migrates up the stack into domain-specific apps, workflows, and services — with major uncertainty about timing, pricing, and which industries will change first.
