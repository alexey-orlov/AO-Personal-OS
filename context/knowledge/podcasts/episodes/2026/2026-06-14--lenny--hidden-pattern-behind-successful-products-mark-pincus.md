## Summary
Mark Pincus explains a repeatable product-playbook—"Proven, Better, New"—that prioritizes copying proven patterns, applying small polishable improvements, and then testing a single novel idea. He ties that framework to concrete product and company rituals (rapid failure, retention metrics, micromanagement when needed) and argues founders should be humble in scope, ruthless about killing hopeful bets, and systematic about distribution and testing—especially as AI lowers build cost but raises the need to test many variants quickly.
