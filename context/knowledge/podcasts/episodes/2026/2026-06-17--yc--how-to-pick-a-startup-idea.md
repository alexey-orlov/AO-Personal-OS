## Summary
John (YC partner) explains a practical rubric for founders who can't choose between startup ideas: stop overthinking, pick one, and commit deeply so you can get real customer signal. He shows how to go deep (burn other boats, learn the customer's business, iterate with product), and what kinds of ideas are especially valuable in the AI era (edge-of-models, verticalized outcome ownership, and ambitious scope).

  - detail: Juggling multiple ideas produces poor, mixed signals that make it impossible to tell what actually works; shallow experimentation leads either to prematurely killing good ideas or to sticking with bad ones. Going deep should feel like adopting a new skin so you generate high-quality data fast.

Reach that level by combining customer conversations with doing the work yourself and iterating product and learning in a tight loop.
