## Summary
The conversation argues that the shift from centralized, legacy media to decentralized new media has fundamentally changed how companies and founders must communicate. Instead of defensive, corporate-sanitized PR, success now requires person-driven, authentic storytelling delivered directly through your own channels and new media voices. Founders should prioritize getting a clear, strategic message before chasing distribution, build teams that can craft narratives, and be willing to be interesting — even polarizing. The panel gives concrete examples (Palantir, SpaceX, Joe Rogan, Instagram/NYT) and practical rules for when to engage, fight, or stay quiet.
