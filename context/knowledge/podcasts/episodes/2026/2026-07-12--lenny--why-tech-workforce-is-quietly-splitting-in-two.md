## Summary
Noam Segal presents results from a 6,000-person annual tech sentiment survey about how AI is changing workers' identities, emotions, and careers. The central argument is that AI has created a sharp bifurcation in tech: roughly half of people feel amplified and energized while the other half feel destabilized, diminished, or deeply uncertain — and that split is driving rising burnout, falling optimism, and concrete management and policy implications.
