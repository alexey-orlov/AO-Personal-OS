## Summary
Eric Ries argues that before joining or building an AI company you must understand governance: legal charters and board structures determine which actors actually hold power and whether a mission survives success. He explains practical governance tools — public benefit corporations, mission guardians, mission-lock vehicles like long-term benefit trusts — and shows with cases (OpenAI, Anthropic, Costco, Novo Nordisk) how those structures either failed or protected a company's ethos. The core throughline: technical quality and product teams matter, but without constitutional structures that resist financial pressure, success itself will corrupt purpose.
