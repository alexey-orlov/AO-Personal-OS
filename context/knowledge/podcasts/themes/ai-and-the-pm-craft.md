## The throughline
AI is rewriting the PM job: when the model can draft a spec or a flow at near-zero cost, the bottleneck moves from producing the artifact to judging it. Two episodes converge on the same shift — taste and evaluation become the scarce skill — and on a concrete process change: write the eval suite first, then build the feature, so quality is measurable from day one. _(synthesis, not evidence)_

## Insights

### AI shifts PMs from spec-writers to taste-makers
As AI drafts specs and flows, the PM's edge moves from producing the artifact to judging it. Jane cites her team cutting spec-writing time 60% but doubling time spent defining 'good', because the model will produce something either way and the real bottleneck becomes taste.

### Write eval suites before building the AI feature
Jane's team writes the evaluation set first — 50 to 100 graded examples — before building an AI feature, so quality is measurable from day one. She says teams that skip this ship demos that work 70% of the time and never close the last 30%.
