## The throughline
AI bends managerial work toward coaching and exception-handling rather than data assembly — at Anthropic, sales managers stopped reconciling spreadsheets and started running action-oriented strategy sessions. The paradox underneath: total human work goes up, not down. As models automate routine tasks, humans spend more time reviewing, fixing, and operating agents — Every doubled headcount while becoming more automated. The role mix shifts; the headcount doesn't drop.

## Insights

### AI-driven forecasts and proposals shift managers to review-and-coach work
AEs use Claude to generate forecasts and proposals—Claude pulls cohort consumption patterns, commit history, product usage, and past negotiation context—so managers inspect and discuss exceptions instead of reconciling spreadsheets. Forecast calls became action-oriented strategy sessions focused on where AEs need help rather than low-level data hygiene. This transitions leadership from data assembly to higher-value coaching, speeding decisions while preserving oversight.

### More automation creates more human work: reviewers and forward-deployed engineers
Shipper calls this the automation paradox: as models automate routine work, humans spend more time reviewing, fixing, and operating agents. Evidence includes Every doubling headcount (15→~30) while becoming more automated, the explosion of pull requests from non-technical users (thousands daily on some agent platforms), and his 'senior engineer benchmark' where GPT-5.5 scored ~62/100 vs. human seniors in the high-80s. So AI increases throughput and sloppy outputs, raising demand for people who curate, integrate, and rewrite work.
