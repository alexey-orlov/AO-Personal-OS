#### 1. 🟡 Wrong pronoun in subordinate clause — 1 instance

When referring to a third party (the client) inside an aside addressed to your interlocutor, the second-person *you* breaks reference. Use **they** for the third party so the listener doesn't have to figure out which "you" you mean.

- "Unless you know that **you** don't use it." → Unless you know that **they** don't use it.

**Drill:** Before using *you* in a clause, ask: am I still talking about the person in front of me, or about a third party I just mentioned? If the latter → *they*.
