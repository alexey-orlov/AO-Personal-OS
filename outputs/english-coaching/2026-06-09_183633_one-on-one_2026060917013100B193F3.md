## Top items to study this session

#### 1. 🟡 Third-person -s on "make(s) sense" — 2 instances

In native English, the closer "Makes sense" carries an implied subject ("[That] makes sense"), so the verb is always third-person singular and takes **-s**. Dropping the -s is a classic Slavic-pattern slip and instantly registers as non-native.

- "**Make** sense." → "**Makes** sense."
- "Ну, **майксен**." → "Ну, **makes** sense." (same pattern, said phonetically earlier in the call)

**Drill:** Treat "Makes sense" / "Makes sense to me" / "That makes sense" as a single frozen unit. Mental check before you say it: *Subject is "[it/that]" → verb takes -s.*
