## Top items to study this session

#### 1. 🟡 Verb pattern: *anticipate* + gerund — 1 instance

After *anticipate*, English uses the **-ing** form, not *to* + infinitive. This is a fixed pattern (like *consider*, *suggest*, *avoid*).

- "accelerator packs that we are building or **anticipate to build**" → "…or **anticipate building**"

**Drill:** *anticipate building · consider doing · suggest trying · avoid saying*. If you can swap in *consider*, use *-ing*.

#### 2. 🟡 Adjective vs. adverb: *real* vs. *really* — 1 instance

Before a noun you need the **adjective** (*real*), not the adverb (*really*). *Really* modifies verbs/adjectives, not nouns.

- "us being **the really vendor** of choice" → "us being **the real vendor** of choice" (or move it: "us **really being** the vendor of choice")

**Drill:** quick test — is the next word a noun? Use *real* (*the real reason*, *the real problem*). Is it a verb/adjective? Use *really* (*really good*, *really works*).

#### 3. 🟡 Demonstrative agreement: *this* → *these* with plurals — 1 instance

*This* is singular; with a plural noun use **these**.

- "we build **this repeatable products**" → "we build **these repeatable products**"

**Drill:** *this product / these products*. Pause on the noun's number before choosing *this*/*these*.

#### 4. 🟡 Missing article — 2 instances

Both a fixed phrase and a singular count-noun need an article here.

- "the process looks like **following**" → "the process looks like **the following**" (or "the process is **as follows**")
- "So **exciting thing** to work on" → "So **it's an exciting thing** to work on"

**Drill:** *as follows* / *the following* are set phrases — they always carry *the*. A singular thing-noun (*thing, point, opportunity*) almost always needs *a/an/the*.

#### 5. 🟡 Tangled / overlong sentences — 2 instances

Grammatical but hard to follow: a doubled "is…is" frame and a 40-word chain that repeats *customers*. Land the main point first, then add the detail.

- "the first point is why it's so exciting to work on that **is us being** the really vendor of choice…" → "the first point: it's exciting **because we're the real vendor of choice**…"

**Drill:** if a sentence has two linking verbs fighting ("the point is why X is Y"), cut one. Say the claim, then explain.
