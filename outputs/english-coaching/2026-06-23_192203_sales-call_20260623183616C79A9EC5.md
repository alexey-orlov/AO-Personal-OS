## Top items to study this session

#### 1. 🟡 Over-long questions that restate the same ask — 2 instances

Your questions are grammatical but sprawl: you pose the ask, then re-pose it, then add a third version. The listener has to wait through all three to answer. Ask once, then stop.

- "how you see the prioritization of that scope … How you internally think about prioritization of stuff that you decide whether you're gonna ship … Do you have any inputs so far …" → **"How do you decide what goes into a package — do you prioritize off pipeline visibility and customer feedback?"**
- "do I get it right that … beyond the MVP is actually driven exclusively by the requests … no magic, no aspirational phase 2, phase 3, phase 4. It's just based on the requests …" → **"So beyond the MVP, is the roadmap driven purely by what you hear in live POCs — no separate aspirational phases?"**

**Drill:** After you finish a question, resist adding "so curious to learn…" or a restated version. One question, one breath, then silence.

#### 2. 🟡 Direct question missing the auxiliary "do" — high-leverage

In a direct *wh-* question you need the auxiliary: **How do you…?**, **What do you…?** Dropping it ("How you think about…?") is one of the clearest non-native tells.

- "**How you internally think** about prioritization…?" → "**How do you internally think** about prioritizing…?"

**Drill:** Wh-word → **do/does** → subject → verb. "How **do** you…", "What **does** the team…".

#### 3. 🟡 Wrong preposition in collocations — 2 instances

Two verbs took the wrong (or an extra) preposition.

- "sorry for **reiterating on** that" → "sorry for **reiterating** that" (*reiterate* is transitive — no preposition)
- "projects that we **collaborate with**" → "projects we **collaborate on**" (*collaborate* **with** people, **on** projects)

**Drill:** *collaborate on a project, with a partner* · *reiterate the point* (no "on").

#### 4. 🟡 Stacked determiners / "quite a huge" — awkward

You can't stack "the" + "a" + an intensifier on one noun, and "different variety" doubles the same idea.

- "given **the quite a huge** variety of **different** vertical use cases" → "given the **huge** variety of vertical use cases"

**Drill:** Pick *one* determiner. "a wide variety" / "the huge variety" — never "the quite a huge".

#### 5. 🟡 Compound-subject pronoun: "you and us" → "you and we" — 1 instance

When the pair is the *subject* of the verb, both pronouns take subject form.

- "the pipelines that **you and us** share" → "the pipelines that **you and we** share"

**Drill:** Drop the other person and test: "…that **we** share" ✓, not "…that **us** share" ✗.

#### 6. 🟡 "kind of a" + an extra article — 1 instance

"a kind of a X" doubles the article. Use either "**a** strategic bet" or "**kind of a** strategic bet" — not both.

- "is **a kind of a** strategic visionary bet" → "is **kind of a** strategic, visionary bet"

**Drill:** "kind of a ___" already carries its article — don't add a second "a" in front.
