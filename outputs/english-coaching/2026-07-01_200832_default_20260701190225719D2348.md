### Top items to study this session

#### 1. 🟡 Missing / extra articles — 5 instances

The most frequent pattern. English needs *the* before a specific referent (*the Bosch case*) and drops the article before abstract/uncountable nouns (*customer satisfaction*, not *the customer satisfaction*). This one rule prevents many future slips.

- "in **Bosch** case, the humans doing work" → in **the Bosch** case
- "especially in **Bosch** case again" → in **the Bosch** case
- "improves **the** customer satisfaction" → improves **customer** satisfaction

**Drill:** specific thing → *the* (*the Bosch case, the pipeline, the pack*). Abstract mass noun → no article (*customer satisfaction, delivery speed, expertise*). Quick check before a noun: "one specific one?" → *the*; "a concept?" → nothing.

#### 2. 🟡 Overlong / run-on sentences — 4 instances

Grammatical but hard to follow: a second idea gets nested inside the first before the first lands. In speech, finish the thought, then start the next one.

- "So yeah, it will be interesting, maybe it's a topic for another conversation, **to, given my focus, to** learn more about your understanding of your priorities and the roadmap…" → "Maybe for another conversation: **given my focus, I'd like to** understand your priorities and roadmap for the packs we're collaborating on."
- "this use case **for if we talk about that in terms of** GPU consumption, it's just about very limited consumption times…" → "**In terms of** GPU consumption, this use case needs very little — about half an hour a week."

**Drill:** one sentence = one idea. When you feel a second "because / given / in terms of" arriving, put a full stop first and start fresh.

#### 3. 🟡 Non-native idioms & set phrases — 4 instances

Real fixed expressions, slightly off. Learn the exact form.

- "to be **on the same side**" → to be **on the same page**
- "**in regards to** the packs" → **with regard to** / **regarding** the packs
- "lots of discovery going on, **on the go**" → lots of discovery happening **on the fly** / **as we go**
- "It's a different **mindset to be turned out**" → It's a different **mindset that has to be adopted**

**Drill:** *on the same page* (aligned) · *with regard to* / *regarding* (about) · *on the fly* (improvised). *On the go* = busy/moving, not "as we go" — don't use it for "gradually."

#### 4. 🟡 Wrong verb form after *help* / *have* / *who* — 3 instances

After *help* use a bare infinitive; after causative *have (someone)* use a bare infinitive; *who* + singular takes *-s*.

- "to help **packaging, productizing** whatever we build" → to help **package and productize**
- "having us **extended** them" → having us **extend** them
- "the partner … who **do** a portion of that and **be** another layer" → who **does** a portion of that and **is** another layer

**Drill:** *help + verb* → base form (*help package, help build*). *have someone + verb* → base form (*have them extend it*). *a partner who does / is*.

#### 5. 🟡 No future tense after "if" — 1 instance

In a conditional, the *if*-clause uses the present, never *will*.

- "if you**'ll** have time" → if you **have** time

**Drill:** *if* + present, main clause + *will* ("If you **have** time, **I'll** send it"). Never *if…will* in the condition.
