# Jumpstart for Product — AI Activation Program for PM Organizations

_v0.6 draft for discussion with Inna / Bogdan · 2026-06-12 · owner: Alex Orlov · one-pager + Appendix A implementation playbook (context-system build/test, underperformance triage, week-by-week W0–W6 + Enablement, 17-row risk register); external sources inline_

## Executive summary

A 6-week Activation Pod (plus Week-0 assessment) that takes one product team from ad-hoc chat use to agent-supported product workflows — then scales by cohort, exactly like the engineering Jumpstart. Same proven spine (assess → baseline → activate → enable → hand over), new payload: the PM workflows — grouped into the four stations below — where product orgs lose time today. The wedge is already in our own data: with coding largely solved by mid-2026, the residual pain Inna names — backlog quality, context management, spec gaps — sits **upstream, in product**. Yet product is the last function clients automate (Telenor: zero AI in product; DAX: one team on Claude, PMs resistant), while PMs gain the most leverage from agents — the gap between "knowing what to build" and "shipping it" is collapsing. Engineering Jumpstart makes delivery faster; this program fixes what then becomes the bottleneck — the quality and speed of what product feeds into delivery. **First deployments are waiting in the existing pipeline:** DAX (PM gap already escalated; relationship in place) and Telenor product teams (explicitly waiting for product-workflow agent ideas; governance angle opens the door).

**Positioning mirror:** clients ask "make our PMs AI-ready"; we answer "make your product operating system AI-ready" — context, artifact hygiene, and governance are the real blockers, not tool skills. (Same reframe as "AI-ready → SDLC-ready" on the engineering side.)

## Where it sits in the SoftServe maturity model (L1–L5)

| Level | Engineering definition | Product equivalent (proposed) |
|---|---|---|
| **L1 Code Assistant** | autocomplete, single-file | ad-hoc ChatGPT/Claude drafting; no shared context, every PM prompts from scratch |
| **L2 Guided Development** | context-aware, KB-connected | skills work from the real stack (Jira/Aha!, Confluence, analytics via MCP); product context system in place |
| **L3 Delegated Engineering** | end-to-end features, human supervises | agents own workflow segments — insight triage, PRD/story drafting, prototype generation — PM reviews and approves |
| **L4 Coordinated Engineering** | multi-agent across SDLC | always-on agents across the product lifecycle: continuous competitor/insight monitoring feeding discovery (W5 plants the first, run under L3 supervision rules); end-state = PM supervises a bounded fleet of human-in-the-loop agents (emerging) |
| **L5 Software Factory** | autonomous, humans set guardrails | out of scope this program |

Clients sit at L1 (often below their own engineering org). **Program target: L1 → L3 for the pilot team, with the L2 foundations built to scale** — W5's always-on agents are L4-shaped capabilities deliberately operated at L3 (we build *toward* L4, we don't hand the pilot autonomy). **Out of scope this program:** cross-agent (A2A) orchestration and fully autonomous pipelines — not yet proven at PM-org level; revisit at L4. Maturity is measured with an extension of Inna's 3×10 questionnaire-to-heatmap framework, adding product dimensions (context readiness, artifact hygiene, insight-flow maturity, governance of customer data). The role-shift the program really sells: PMs move from *authoring artifacts* to *designing the guardrails, decision-rights, and evals agents run inside* — the constitution, approval tiers, and eval contracts in Appendix A are that shift made concrete.

## What we automate — the PM workflow map

Grouped into four stations; each gets skills built on the client's actual tool stack during the pod:

1. **Context foundation** — the product context system: a versioned repo of strategy, positioning, personas, metric definitions, voice + MCP connections to the system of record (Jira/Aha!/Productboard, Confluence/Notion, analytics, call libraries). Built FIRST — context quality, not prompt skill, is what makes every downstream workflow work. And PMs get **configured defaults, not blank tools** — pre-built skills, rules, and connections per role; the distribution pattern behind HubSpot's and Shopify's adoption curves, and how Anthropic PM-built workflows spread internally (packaged as team plugins).
2. **Discovery & insight** — customer insights triage (support tickets, interviews, NPS), market research briefs, competitor monitoring, product analytics → automated "what changed and what to do" TLDRs instead of dashboard archaeology. This is the largest documented unmet need: PMs use AI for research at ~5% but want it at ~32% — a +27pp gap, the biggest of any PM task (Lenny's 1,750-respondent survey).
3. **Definition & delivery** — PRD/epic shaping, feature breakdown, story + AC writing (reuses the proven ADLC Define-phase skills: PM Shape Epic / Shape Features / Write Story), and **prototype-first definition**: PM-built working prototypes to ~70–80% replace long PRDs as the alignment artifact where suitable, with a short companion doc carrying metrics and guardrails. For clients also running ADLC, the frontier step: product artifacts move to git — **PRDs versioned like code, the spec as the source of truth agents execute against** (the GitHub Spec-Kit / LaunchDarkly pattern — in early production use, practitioner-reported).
4. **Communication & ops** — stakeholder updates, release communication, personal productivity, product knowledge base maintenance. Anything customer- or exec-facing ships only on named human approval.

The map applies to both customer-facing and internal product orgs. Week 0 weights depth per station against the pilot's actual pain — the context foundation is always built; the weekly plan below is the full-coverage template, trimmed rather than stretched. Everything runs on the **live backlog from week one — no sandbox pilots**: MIT's GenAI Divide traces the 95% pilot-failure rate to exactly that gap (no workflow integration, no aligned incentives, tools that never learn the org).

## Program structure

**Week 0 — Express Product Assessment** (reuses the Express SDLC Assessment pattern: intake form + 3–4 working sessions + readout). Outputs: maturity heatmap, ranked pain points, pilot team + use cases, **readiness gate** — frozen metric definitions and 2 sprints of baseline data, system-of-record hygiene confirmed (AC fields, backlog structure), licensing + data-handling sign-off with legal aligned to default-to-yes ("how do we do this safely," not "whether") — the unglamorous unblock behind the fastest corporate adoption curves. Hard lesson from DAX: no baseline = unprovable impact; and skills are customized to the client's actual stack (DAX escalated because workshops talked Jira while the client ran Aha!).

**Weeks 1–6 — Activation Pod** (one pilot product team / PM triad). Day-level activities, the build-and-test method for the context system, and the underperforming-workflow triage playbook are in **[Appendix A](#appendix-a--implementation-playbook-how-we-build-test-and-recover)** — the week summaries below are the spine; the appendix is the operating manual.
- **W1** Foundations: build the product context system, connect MCPs, intro training (incl. the core working discipline: roll context in, force clarifying questions, block premature deliverables — treat the model like an eager junior), baseline snapshot, friction log opened.
- **W2** Definition station live: epic/PRD/story skills on the real backlog; champion pairing sessions per PM.
- **W3** Discovery & insight station: triage pipeline + research/competitor skills; analytics TLDR.
- **W4** Prototype-first definition + communication station (human-approval gates configured).
- **W5** Automation: always-on agents — weekly competitor digest, insight triage, metrics TLDR — designed with persistent memory and parallel fan-out where useful (the adoption-analytics / sentiment-monitoring agent archetypes Anthropic PMs run internally); handoff handshake into engineering (pre-grooming contract — composable with engineering Jumpstart/ADLC if present).
- **W6** Measure, report, hand over: metric delta vs. baseline, outcomes report, playbook, Go/No-Go to Enablement.

Each week has a written Go/No-Go signal owned by the team champion (same mechanics as the ADLC rollout: champions, weekly micro-retros, friction log, pairing sessions).

**Adoption design for resistant PMs** (the known failure mode): pick the pilot team from early adopters, not skeptics — wins pull the rest; participating PMs (champions included) get protected 10–15% AI time per sprint as a participation prerequisite (champions carry ~2–3 hrs/wk of coaching load on top); skeptics convert on demonstrated time-back, not training hours. Same plays as the leaders/laggards research, applied to product roles. Two field-proven levers on top: an **individual 4-tier AI-fluency rubric** per PM (unacceptable → capable → adoptive → transformative; Zapier runs hiring and onboarding on it — the person-level complement to the org-level L1–L5), and an **explicit sponsor mandate** — reflexive AI use stated as a baseline expectation and echoed in review cycles (the Shopify memo pattern; its maximal form gates new headcount on "show AI can't do this job").

**Then — Enablement cohorts**: 4–5 product teams per cohort, 4–6 weeks each, champions coach adoption, optional hackathon (Zapier's took company-wide usage 10%→50% in one week). Adoption stays owned by **line product leads, not a central AI team** — the consistent success pattern in the failure research. Plan for the documented curve: rollouts that skip the foundations plateau at 15–20% (practitioner-reported/directional); past ~60% adoption skepticism *resurges*, and the playbook flips to peer demo videos and trend data rather than louder mandates (HubSpot's curve — from its engineering org; treat the *shape* as transferable, expect PM adoption to start slower per our Telenor/DAX read, and **set cohort expectation bands below the pilot's measured delta** since the pilot is a cherry-picked early-adopter team — risk #17). Steady state: Working Group owns the skills library **with explicit kill criteria** — skills and agents that stop earning usage get sunset on schedule, the planned version of what otherwise happens unplanned (Gartner: >40% of agentic-AI projects canceled by 2027 on cost and unclear value). No consulting dependency.

## Metrics (baseline 2 sprints before; never rank individuals; report quarterly)

| Category | Metrics | Target / guardrail |
|---|---|---|
| Adoption (leading) | skill adoption rate per workflow; % artifacts AI-assisted | ≥80% within 3 sprints |
| Velocity | requirements cycle time (idea → dev-ready); documentation throughput; time-to-insight; prototype turnaround | ↑ vs. baseline |
| Quality | AI-draft rework rate; pre-grooming readiness rate; mid-sprint clarification requests | rework ≤10–15% |
| Experience & business | PM DX score; time saved per role/sprint | DX ≥4.0/5 |
| **Guardrails** | downstream defect escape, mid-sprint scope change | must not degrade — speed at the cost of spec quality is the failure mode |

Calibrate sponsor expectations with field numbers: realistically AI compresses PM execution work ~15–25% on specific task classes today, not 10× — **our calibration band, triangulated from DORA, METR, and Lenny's 1,750-respondent survey** (which found 63% of PMs save 4+ hrs/week, not a 10× multiplier). Self-reports overstate gains — METR's RCT (16 OSS devs on coding tasks) found developers who *felt* 20% faster measured 19% *slower*; we carry the felt-vs-measured *gap* to PM work, not the 19% magnitude — hence telemetry over testimony. Pair telemetry with the DX survey — divergence between "what happened" and "how it feels" is the single most informative adoption signal. Expect the J-curve: a productivity dip with enablement, much longer without (DORA's ROI report; dip depth/duration figures are in the full report). Make the Wave-1 regression explicit in the governance narrative or one bad sprint kills a good skill.

## Governance & processes set up (the "basic processes" deliverable)

Extends the existing visibility / policy / enforcement operating model to product surfaces:
- **Visibility:** AI tool inventory incl. shadow AI in product roles; usage telemetry.
- **Policy:** risk tiers (T1 drafting assistant → T3 agent with write access to roadmap/customer comms); data classification for customer interviews, support tickets, PII in insight pipelines; human-in-the-loop checkpoints per tier; AI never publishes externally without a named approver.
- **Enforcement:** approved-tools registry, secret/PII scanning on prompts, audit trail, kill switch; token spend **visible per pod from day one, but no punitive caps during activation** — meter anxiety kills adoption faster than overspend (Shopify runs uncapped budgets with a public usage leaderboard). The discipline is watching both meters — productivity and cost — together; caps come at steady state, not during the learning curve. Treat each always-on agent as a **scoped non-human identity** with its own least-privilege, individually-revocable credential (not a shared PM token), so the kill switch and leakage controls operate per-agent.

EU AI Act governance readiness is a door-opener for EU product orgs (proven at Telenor) — but pitch it right: the **November 2025 Digital Omnibus on AI** postponed Annex III high-risk obligations from 2 Aug 2026 to 2 Dec 2027 (provisional political agreement reached May 2026, pending formal adoption), so the hook is *getting ahead of the Dec-2027 deadline* and general EU AI-governance readiness, not an imminent-August cliff (note: Art. 50 *transparency* obligations stay live 2 Aug 2026 — only the Annex III high-risk obligations moved).

## Engagement team & buyer

**Pod:** Product Lead 0.5 FTE (offering owner) + 1–2 Intelligence Engineers + Product Ops/BA 0.5. This fixes the recurring "wrong 4th seat" failure on engineering pods — product expertise is in the core team, not an afterthought. Pod effort is allocated on BCG's 10-20-70 rule: ~70% goes to workflow redesign, enablement, and adoption design — not to tools and prompts.
**Buyer today:** CTO + VPs (existing relationships; the named first deployments above enter through them). **CPO direct motion = open GTM question** — product orgs buy separately from CTOs; the PE-portfolio productivity mandate (GI Partners: +35% by EOY 2026 — per the 2026-06-11 walkthrough, not publicly verifiable) is the likely entry argument.

# Appendix A — Implementation playbook (how we build, test, and recover)

_The week summaries in the body are the spine; this is the operating manual — what the pod actually does, how the context system gets built and tested, and what we do when a workflow underperforms. Citations are inline. Maturity is flagged: practices are **proven** in named production use or a study unless marked **(emerging)** = early/practitioner-reported or **(our heuristic)** = our reasoned extrapolation from a proven base, not yet an industry standard. We mark the heuristics honestly so the client knows what is battle-tested vs. what is our informed judgment._

## A1. How the product context system is built & validated

### A1.1 Build sequence — do this in order (W0 → W1, compounding after)

1. **Gate the slice (W0).** Before any build, confirm the pilot workflow is a fit: UI-visible or artifact-shaped, low cross-team dependency, off the regulated/audit-critical path, with a clean enough source-of-truth to ground on. If not, scope a thinner slice. _(our heuristic, layered on Anthropic's proven prototype-first base — [Aakash Gupta on Anthropic PMs building first](https://aakashgupta.medium.com/anthropic-pms-dont-write-prds-they-build-the-first-version-themselves-it-s-working-2dd749420870))_
2. **Error-analysis first (W0).** Pull ~50–100 real artifacts the team already produces (past PRDs, groomings, insight notes); the pilot PM open-codes the **first failure** in each; cluster into a frequency-ranked taxonomy; stop when ~20 traces add no new category. The top 2–3 clusters become the eval targets. In Hamel Husain's NurtureBoss case, 3 issue types accounted for >60% of failures. ([Hamel Husain — field guide](https://hamel.dev/blog/posts/field-guide/), [evals FAQ](https://hamel.dev/blog/posts/evals-faq/))
3. **Root file (W1 day 1).** Hand-write or `/init` a **short** root `AGENTS.md` (vendor-neutral, adopted across 60k+ repos): context (role, product, ICP, OKRs, terminology) · writing rules + banned words · output standards (force clarifying questions, metrics-with-baselines, risks-with-mitigations). Make `CLAUDE.md`/`.cursor/rules` a one-line pointer to it. **Keep it short** — bloated root files make the model ignore real instructions; for each line ask "would removing this cause a mistake?" ([Anthropic — context engineering](https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/effective-context-engineering-for-ai-agents), [Builder.io — AGENTS.md](https://www.builder.io/blog/agents-md), [Claude Code best practices](https://code.claude.com/docs/en/best-practices)) _(the specific "<300 lines" figure is community lore, not an Anthropic number — cite the principle, not the count.)_
4. **3-tier knowledge tree (W1)** on the client's real repo/vault: **Tier 1** root file loaded every session · **Tier 2** per-folder index files ("a map of where each kind of info lives") · **Tier 3** content files retrieved just-in-time. Author the foundational Tier-3 set: `product-strategy.md`, `icp-and-personas.md`, `metric-definitions.md` (kept **separate** from `queries/` and `schemas/`), `glossary.md`, `voice-and-writing-rules.md`, `decision-log.md`, plus 2–3 anonymized past PRDs as gold-standard examples. ([Anthropic — context engineering](https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/effective-context-engineering-for-ai-agents); the analytics-folder split is from a single-practitioner production account — [Aakash Gupta — Claude Code team OS](https://www.news.aakashg.com/p/claude-code-team-os))
5. **Package recurring work as Skills, not prose (W1).** A skill = a folder whose `SKILL.md` frontmatter is the only thing loaded until it's relevant, so the bundled context is effectively unbounded. Build `prd-writer`, `competitive-analysis`, `metrics-definer`, `launch-checklist`, `user-research-synthesis`; each asks 3–5 clarifying questions and flags gaps with `[NEED: …]` rather than inventing. ([Anthropic — Agent Skills](https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/equipping-agents-for-the-real-world-with-agent-skills))
6. **Add a `constitution.md` (W1–W2)** of non-negotiable product principles + decision rights. The Spec-Kit chain — `/speckit.constitution` → `/speckit.specify` (what+why) → `/speckit.plan` (how, validated against the constitution) → `/speckit.tasks` (commands are namespaced in current Spec-Kit) — maps cleanly to discovery → PRD → delivery and gives a deterministic anti-drift gate. ([GitHub Spec-Kit](https://github.com/github/spec-kit), [Microsoft — spec-driven development](https://developer.microsoft.com/blog/spec-driven-development-spec-kit))
7. **Wire MCPs as configured defaults, not blank tools (W1).** `claude mcp add` for Atlassian (Jira+Confluence), Notion/Productboard, Linear/Asana, Amplitude/Pendo; then **pre-seed** saved JQL, default spaces, analytics views. At HubSpot, adoption "shot above 50% overnight" once it **lifted restrictions and gave everyone a seat**; the curated MCP server + shared rules on every machine were **part of the configured-defaults push** that — alongside peer-validation + quantitative proof — later carried adoption past the ~60% wall to 90%; so pre-seed defaults **and** distribute access, and don't hand the pilot bare connectors. At cohort scale, route through a central LLM proxy for cost/analytics/failover (Shopify "Genie"). **Security design gate at wiring time:** any agent that combines private-data access + exposure to untrusted content + an outbound/write path sits in the *lethal trifecta* — force a human-approval gate or sever one leg (e.g. read-only roadmap, no outbound) before it goes live (ties to risk #9 + the W6 untrusted-skill rule). ([HubSpot — Context is Key](https://product.hubspot.com/blog/context-is-key-how-hubspot-scaled-ai-adoption), [Bessemer — Shopify playbook](https://www.bvp.com/atlas/inside-shopifys-ai-first-engineering-playbook), [Simon Willison — the lethal trifecta](https://simonwillison.net/2025/Jun/16/the-lethal-trifecta/), [OWASP LLM01](https://genai.owasp.org/llmrisk/llm01-prompt-injection/))
8. **Tier the model to the task (W1 — our heuristic on a proven base).** Set a default model per skill in its frontmatter: route high-volume, low-stakes work (ticket/insight triage, classification, summarization) to a fast/cheap model and reserve a frontier reasoning model for PRD shaping, strategy, and ambiguous discovery; re-check the routing against the in-repo eval on every model upgrade, and tie spend to the per-pod token meter. Cache the stable Tier-1 prefix to cut repeat-call cost/latency. ([Anthropic — context engineering](https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/effective-context-engineering-for-ai-agents) for JIT; [Anthropic — prompt caching](https://docs.claude.com/en/docs/build-with-claude/prompt-caching) for the cache; the per-task model-tiering itself is our heuristic)
9. **Install the capture loop (W1, continuous).** End every session asking the agent "what did we learn today worth documenting?" and fold it into the right tier file — the library compounds instead of being a one-time build. ([Claude Code best practices](https://code.claude.com/docs/en/best-practices))

### A1.2 How we test it — the "good enough to proceed" bar (answers "how will it be tested?")

- **Golden set.** From the W0 clusters, build a **20–50 case** set (Anthropic: 20–50 real-failure tasks is a strong start; early dev has large effect sizes so small samples suffice). Sizes scale with scope: ~20–50 foundational, up to ~100 for the full Definition scorecard (W2), ~20 per always-on agent (W5). You can synthesize realistic *inputs* with zero users; write a known-good **reference solution** per case to prove the grader is configured right. Our quality bar (stricter than Hamel's single-SME default): **two SMEs independently reach the same pass/fail** — if not, rewrite the case. ([Anthropic — demystifying evals](https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/demystifying-evals-for-ai-agents), [Hamel — evals](https://hamel.dev/blog/posts/evals/))
- **Three graders, cheap-first.** (1) **Code assertions** every commit — required PRD sections present, no PII/UUID leakage, links resolve, Jira fields populated. (2) **LLM-as-judge** for fuzzy outputs. (3) **Human SME** spot-check for the rest. **Grade the outcome, not the path.** ([Anthropic — demystifying evals](https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/demystifying-evals-for-ai-agents))
- **LLM-judge for fuzzy PM outputs (emerging).** Write a 4–8 criterion rubric scored *objectively* from the text (success metrics defined? each persona has a stated pain? acceptance criteria present? concise?) — not "captures user needs well." Use G-Eval (judge reasons before scoring); for iteration run **pairwise vs a baseline, order swapped-and-averaged** to kill position bias; add a concision criterion to fight verbosity bias; use a cross-family judge to fight self-preference. ([rubric/G-Eval methods](https://medium.com/@adnanmasood/rubric-based-evals-llm-as-a-judge-methodologies-and-empirical-validation-in-domain-context-71936b989e80))
- **Calibrate the judge before trusting it.** Binary pass/fail (not 1–5 — raters cluster at the middle); have **3+ PMs label a 50-case set**, run the judge on the same set, target **75–90% judge–human agreement**. Treat a **100% pass rate as a red flag** (eval too easy). ([Hamel — evals FAQ](https://hamel.dev/blog/posts/evals-faq/), [Confident AI](https://www.confident-ai.com/blog/why-llm-as-a-judge-is-the-best-llm-evaluation-method))
- **Capability → regression.** Hard "capability" tasks start at low pass rate; as they're solved, graduate them into a **regression suite gated near 100%** that catches backsliding on any prompt/tool/model change. For the always-on agents (W5) report **pass@k _and_ pass^k** — 75%/trial across 3 trials is only ~42% all-pass, so reliability needs repeated runs (scoped rigor for the autonomous agents, not blanket overhead on every skill). ([Anthropic — demystifying evals](https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/demystifying-evals-for-ai-agents))
- **CI in days, not an enterprise tool.** **DeepEval** = "pytest for LLMs": parametrize over the golden set, `assert_test`, wire `deepeval test run` into a GitHub Action on every push + nightly, mark a baseline. Pair with a tracking layer (Braintrust auto-posts PR diffs; promptfoo adds red-team vectors — relevant since PM agents touch Jira/Confluence/customer data). Build a throwaway annotation viewer in ~half a day; **budget 60–80% of effort to error analysis + eval, not infra.** **Default spine:** DeepEval in a GitHub Action; add Braintrust (PR-diff tracking) and promptfoo (red-team) only if the pod has time. The eval-as-tests apparatus is proven in coding-agent/LLM-app contexts and deliberately transplanted to PM artifacts — the LLM-judge layer is (emerging), de-risked by the W0 error-analysis golden set + the 75–90% judge-human calibration. ([DeepEval CI/CD](https://deepeval.com/docs/evaluation-unit-testing-in-ci-cd), [Braintrust](https://www.braintrust.dev/articles/best-ai-evals-tools-cicd-2025), [Hamel — evals FAQ](https://hamel.dev/blog/posts/evals-faq/))
- **Acceptance bar.** Not subjective polish — a runnable check: give the agent a real PM question ("draft the PRD for X using our metric defs + ICP") it must answer correctly **pulling only the right Tier-3 files**, with an **adversarial fresh-context subagent** reviewing the result against the spec. ([Claude Code best practices](https://code.claude.com/docs/en/best-practices))
- **Online evals (the production half).** Offline CI catches regressions on known cases; once agents are live (W5), run inline LLM-judge + code-assertion scorers on a **sampled % of real production traces** (same rubric) so regressions on real inputs surface continuously — instrument to the OpenTelemetry GenAI semantic conventions (emerging — the OTel GenAI spec is still stabilizing) so the trace store is portable. ([OpenTelemetry — GenAI semantic conventions](https://opentelemetry.io/docs/specs/semconv/gen-ai/))
- **What we defer, openly.** *KPI-level* A/B gating on real business metrics (Intercom-grade) needs traffic volume the pod lacks — we **name it the client's post-handover milestone**; trace-level online scoring + the golden set + assertions + calibrated judge + CI gate ship now. ([Intercom/OpenAI case](https://openai.com/index/intercom/))

## A2. When a workflow underperforms — the triage playbook (answers "what if some don't perform well?")

**Hard rule: reproduce the first failure in isolation before changing anything; check the model _last_.** Teams burn days swapping models when the real cause was a bad input, a saturated window, or an unclear prompt. ([Lenny's × Hamel — evals, error analysis & better prompts](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/evals-error-analysis-and-better-prompts))

**Decision tree:** run error analysis on ~100 real traces → open-code the **first** failure (upstream errors cascade; fixing the first often clears the rest) → then isolate the class **in order**: **1. Input?** (bad ticket / missing context in the source artifact — fix the source) → **2. Context?** (saturation / wrong retrieval / distractors — §A2.1) → **3. Prompt/skill?** (cherry-pick the targeted fix, re-eval vs frozen baseline) → **4. Model** (last resort). ([Hamel — error analysis method](https://hamel.dev/blog/posts/evals-faq/why-is-error-analysis-so-important-in-llm-evals-and-how-is-it-performed.html)) _(the taxonomy→component mapping is our faithful paraphrase of Hamel's teaching; the ~100/20 trace counts are his explicit rough heuristic, not a validated threshold.)_ For multi-step agents, build a **transition failure matrix** (rows = last good state, cols = where it first broke) to see which hop dominates before debugging the whole flow. ([Hamel — agentic workflows](https://hamel.dev/blog/posts/evals-faq/how-do-i-evaluate-agentic-workflows.html))

**A2.1 Context saturation is real and measurable.** Chroma's 18-model study found quality degrades as input grows even on trivial tasks; distractors compound; and near the limit the failure *mode* differed by family (some models abstained, others continued confidently). So **don't dump the whole corpus in.** Remedies: compaction (summarize history, keep decisions/open bugs) — incl. Claude's GA **context-editing / automatic compaction** that auto-clears stale tool results — just-in-time retrieval (store Jira keys/URLs, load full objects at runtime), structured note-taking outside the window (Claude's file-based **memory tool**, vendor-GA; richer vector stores emerging/out-of-scope), sub-agents returning 1–2K-token summaries, and **one-task-one-session** discipline. ([Chroma — context rot](https://www.trychroma.com/research/context-rot), [Anthropic — context engineering](https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/effective-context-engineering-for-ai-agents))

**A2.2 Version-pin + cherry-pick, never blind roll-forward.** Version the whole artifact (prompt + tool schemas + temperature); keep a tagged last-known-good with one-click rollback; promote a changed skill through a staging eval against the current prod version on the same trace set. Fix the specific thing error analysis pointed to — don't rewrite the skill and hope. ([Maxim — prompt versioning](https://www.getmaxim.ai/articles/prompt-versioning-and-its-best-practices-2025/))

**A2.3 Recover before you kill — restraint gate & graduated autonomy (emerging).** The "eager junior" pattern: instruct the agent to reason, surface options + trade-offs, and **wait for explicit approval** — this alone recovers many over-reaching workflows (the failure was unchecked autonomy, not capability). Start every workflow approval-gated/read-only; promote to autonomous only when the in-repo eval clears the regression-suite bar and pass^k holds across N trials (§A1.2); **on underperformance — sampled production traces below the bar — demote one rung** as the default reversible recovery. ([AICE Labs — over-eager intern](https://www.aicelabs.com/articles/ai-your-overeager-intern), [HITL→HOTL](https://bytebridge.medium.com/from-human-in-the-loop-to-human-on-the-loop-evolving-ai-agent-autonomy-c0ae62c3bf91))

**A2.4 Kill / sunset criteria, set at ship time.** Each skill ships with an owner, a metric, a threshold (declining usage, rising override/edit rate, no measured time saved), and a documented manual fallback. When tripped, deprecate through the **same governed path that launched it** (revoke access, archive data, capture learnings) so no "ghost agents" linger. This is the discipline behind Gartner's forecast that **>40% of agentic-AI projects are canceled by end-2027** on cost/unclear-value — dropping a no-ROI workflow to manual *deliberately* is what separates the surviving 60%. ([OneReach — agent lifecycle](https://onereach.ai/blog/agent-lifecycle-management-stages-governance-roi/), [Gartner](https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2025-06-25-gartner-predicts-over-40-percent-of-agentic-ai-projects-will-be-canceled-by-end-of-2027))

## A3. Week-by-week — activities, exit gate & mitigations

Mirrors SoftServe's own model (Assessment → Activation Pod → Large-Scale Training). Each week: **Do · Exit gate · Watch-outs.**

**W0 — Assessment & baseline.** _(body W0)_
- **Do:** self-score each PM on the Zapier 4-tier fluency rubric (Unacceptable/Capable/Adoptive/Transformative), target +1 tier by W6 measured by demonstrated workflow change ([Zapier](https://zapier.com/blog/raising-ai-fluency-bar-in-hiring/)) _(Zapier uses this for hiring/development; the fixed W0→W6 gate is our adaptation)_ · pick ONE workflow × ONE persona × ONE data domain, audit backlog quality first · write pilot charter + RACI (incl. a **named backup sponsor** + a written exec mandate, not one person's verbal buy-in) · **capture a frozen baseline as medians over a 2-sprint window + a DX-style experience survey** (the qualitative part can't be reconstructed later) · set a written **15–25% expectation band** with the sponsor, not "10×" (band per the DORA/METR/Lenny's triangulation in the body; [EY](https://www.ey.com/en_gl/newsroom/2025/11/ey-survey-reveals-companies-are-missing-out-on-up-to-40-percent-of-ai-productivity-gains-due-to-gaps-in-talent-strategy) backs the *expectation-gap* point — orgs miss up to 40% of gains to talent-strategy gaps) · governance: 4-tier data-classification map, provision client-tenant enterprise seats with no-training (request Zero-Data-Retention for sensitive data), **block personal Free/Pro at SSO/network** ([Anthropic Enterprise](https://www.anthropic.com/product/enterprise)) · run the error-analysis pass (§A1.1).
- **Exit:** signed charter + approved data access; frozen baseline + survey captured; expectation band documented; classification map + enterprise tenancy + personal-account block live; fitness gate passed.
- **Watch-outs:** garbage-in → audit context quality, scope thin · no baseline → make capture a hard gate (DAX had none 2 months in) · personal-account leakage (Samsung-mode: source code leaked to ChatGPT, [incident](https://incidentdatabase.ai/cite/768/)) → enterprise no-training tools + classification gate.

**W1 — Kickoff, context system, community.** _(body W1)_
- **Do:** 2-hour hands-on lab, optionally a 1-day mini-hackathon to spike usage (Zapier's hackathon took daily AI use <10%→>50% in a week, [GTMnow](https://gtmnow.com/gtm-185-zapier-ceo-ai-adoption-hackathon-playbook/)) · build the context system + first skills on the **real backlog** (§A1.1) · name **1 champion + 1 deputy** per 8–12 PMs (a single champion is a single point of failure across risks #3/#5/#11/#13) · stand up the durable infra that made Zapier stick: a low-judgment #ai-pm-help channel, office hours, a copy-pasteable prompt/use-case library with failure notes · build the **AI-assist tagging convention** (custom field per PRD/spec marking it AI-assisted + which skill) — no tag at creation = no attribution later. **Day shape:** D1 root file + tree scaffold · D2 Tier-3 + 2 gold PRDs · D3 first 2 skills + MCP wiring · D4 model-tiering + capture loop · D5 lightweight acceptance check.
- **Exit:** context system passes a **lightweight W1 acceptance check** (the formal scorecard is built in W2, §A1.2); champion + deputy named; community + library live; tagging in place; **≥80% of pilot PMs ran ≥1 real-backlog skill this week** (logged via the AI-assist tag).
- **Watch-outs:** hackathon spike decays → pair with durable infra · blank-MCP friction → ship configured defaults · champion overload → cap at 2–3 hrs/wk, scope out tech support.

**W2 — Definition station + eval scorecard + guardrails.** _(body W2)_
- **Do:** build the **Definition-station skills on the real backlog** — PRD/epic/story shaping (the proven ADLC Define-phase skills) — i.e. the first MVP agent flow (input → retrieval/generation → output → feedback capture) · connect the full stack via MCPs (one Linear MCP = 25+ tools; Markdown-export path for Aha!/Productboard) ([Aakash — advanced CC for PMs](https://www.aakashg.com/advanced-claude-code-for-product-managers-from-mcps-to-github-automation/)) · stand up the **PRD-as-code** repo (version-controlled Markdown PRDs the agent reads) ([ChatPRD workflows](https://www.chatprd.ai/how-i-ai/3-workflows-to-make-your-codebase-ai-ready-with-claire-vo-zach-davis)) · `specify init` + author the client's `constitution.md` · build the **eval scorecard** (cases from the W0 error-analysis clusters; see the A1.2 sizing ladder) + basic prompt-injection red-team ([promptfoo](https://qaskills.sh/blog/promptfoo-complete-guide-2026)) · wire **PII redaction** (Presidio gateway hook) benchmarked on the client's own traffic + **Gitleaks** pre-commit / **TruffleHog** history sweep ([Presidio+LiteLLM](https://docs.litellm.ai/docs/tutorials/presidio_pii_masking)).
- **Exit:** Definition skills produce sprint-ready drafts on real tickets; eval scorecard populated; constitution authored; redaction live; secret scanning in CI.
- **Watch-outs:** context rot → context budget + JIT retrieval + fresh sessions (§A2.1) · redaction over/under-blocks → tiered hybrid, benchmark first (no method is both high-precision and high-recall) · secrets committed → Gitleaks/TruffleHog + rotation runbook (matches the repo's never-commit-secrets rule).

**W3 — Discovery & insight station.** _(body W3)_ — the +27pp unmet-need wedge, and the dedicated week for the body's headline workflow.
- **Do:** build the **insight-triage pipeline** over the real corpus (support tickets, interview transcripts, NPS) → themed, deduped, frequency-ranked insights (same open-coding → frequency-ranked-taxonomy method as the W0 error-analysis pass, A1.1 step 2) · a **competitor/market monitor** · the **analytics-TLDR** ("what changed and what to do," not dashboard archaeology) · because synthesis output is fuzzy, gate it with the **LLM-judge rubric + SME spot-check** (§A1.2), not code assertions · **de-identify/aggregate customer data at ingest** (GDPR minimization) — feed de-identified themes, not raw archives, into agent context.
- **Exit:** triage pipeline runs on the real backlog with judged quality above the rubric bar; analytics-TLDR live; competitor monitor returning useful deltas; customer data de-identified at ingest.
- **Watch-outs:** fuzzy-output drift → LLM-judge rubric + SME review, not vibes · customer-data exposure → de-identify at ingest, themes-not-archives · "research feels automatable but isn't fully" → PM stays the decision-maker; the agent surfaces candidates, doesn't conclude.

**W4 — Prototype-first definition + communication + trust calibration.** _(body W4)_
- **Do:** build the pilot feature **prototype-first** — PM takes it to a functional prototype with the agent (Figma/image → working prototype; auto-accept loops from a clean git state so changes are revertable), engineer does the production last mile; write the PRD after as the companion doc ([Claude — product development in the agentic era](https://claude.com/blog/product-development-in-the-agentic-era)) _(the "PM to 70–80%, engineer finishes, PRD-after" split is our gloss on Anthropic's qualitative "functional prototype in a sitting"; it's a single vendor's internal practice, not a cross-org study)_ · for high-ambiguity work run the full Spec-Kit pipeline with **`/speckit.analyze` as the pre-implementation gate** that blocks constitution violations · stand up the **communication/stakeholder-update skills** (the body's Comms & ops station) · run a **trust-calibration session both directions** — where to stop over-verifying AND where to stop over-trusting (high AI-trust correlates with reduced critical-thinking effort, [Lee et al. CHI 2025](https://dl.acm.org/doi/full/10.1145/3706598.3713778)) · recalibrate from the **categorized friction log** (inaccurate/unsafe/format/missing-context/performance).
- **Exit:** functional prototype + companion PRD; `/speckit.analyze` passing with no violations; comms skills live; friction log categorized; human-validation gate enforced.
- **Watch-outs:** prototype-first collapses on a non-AI-ready codebase → gated in W0, scope thin · regulated/cross-team/no-UI work → carve out, use the spec-driven flow instead · over-reliance/atrophy → require PMs to log overrides/rejections.

**W5 — Always-on agents + verification discipline + a measured win.** _(body W5: automation)_
- **Do:** build two always-on PM agents on the orchestrator-worker pattern — (1) adoption-analytics with persistent memory, (2) competitor/sentiment monitor with 3–5 parallel subagents — with task-complexity scaling rules; reserve fan-out for genuinely parallel work (Anthropic's multi-agent beat single-agent by 90% on their research eval **but at ~15× the tokens of a plain chat**, [Anthropic — multi-agent system](https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/multi-agent-research-system)) · **engineer memory as an architecture decision, not a product:** durable memory for these bounded agents = the versioned Tier-3 context files + `decision-log.md` + a per-agent scratchpad, with Claude's GA context-editing + file-based memory tool as the persistence primitive (NOT a bespoke external vector store) — memory, not raw model capability, increasingly gates long-horizon reliability; richer episodic/semantic stores are (emerging) and out of pilot scope · **ship each agent WITH its eval** (~20-query real-usage set, LLM-judge + human spot-check, kept in-repo so it re-runs on model upgrades) · turn on **run-level tracing** (inputs, tool calls, retrievals, outputs, token/latency per run) so W6's fresh-traces cadence has a store to read · demo the **background-agent** pattern (scoped Markdown PRD + screenshot + tight prompt → cloud agent → **PR for review, never auto-merge**) · build a **mandatory verify-then-ship ritual** with explicitly budgeted review time — the verification tax can exceed the time AI saves, and may not shrink (can grow) as models produce more plausible output ([METR RCT](https://metr.org/blog/2025-07-10-early-2025-ai-experienced-os-dev-study/)) _(METR proves the base; the "grows as models improve" coupling is emerging)_ · **engineer a measured win NOW** — practitioner analysis puts the quit point at weeks 5–8 once novelty fades and payoff lags ([AI Smart Ventures — week six](https://aismartventures.com/posts/ai-adoption-curves-why-week-six-is-when-teams-quit/)) (emerging) · **J-curve watch:** pre-warn team + sponsor that throughput may dip before it rises (DORA 2024: +25% adoption ↔ −1.5% throughput, −7.2% delivery stability, while 75% *feel* more productive; [RedMonk](https://redmonk.com/rstephens/2024/11/26/dora2024/)) _(correlational; the week-level timing is our extrapolation — DORA gives no week-level timeline)._
- **Exit:** agents pass their eval + spot-check; tracing on; background pattern demoed with PRs landing; verification ritual live; a measured win landed while support is present; **pilot team ≥80% of target workflows AI-assisted** (on track to the body's ≥80%-in-3-sprints target); team briefed on the J-curve.
- **Watch-outs:** agents trusted before validated → no-eval-no-ship gate · fan-out cost/misfit → cap subagent counts · vague background prompts → standardize PRD+screenshot+AC, always-PR · **connector/auth failure on always-on agents** (OAuth expiry, rate-limit throttling, silent schema change) → health-check + auth-expiry alerting + documented manual fallback, tied to run-level tracing · week-6 quit → land the win while support is present · J-curve misread as failure → report quarterly, pre-educate.

**W6 — Measure, ROI, hand over & train-the-trainer.** _(body W6)_
- **Do:** compute the **metric delta vs the W0 baseline** + **ROI as a stress-tested range** — time-saved/case × cases/month × loaded PM rate, and if a CFO number is needed run the DORA ROI calculator with **telemetry-informed inputs (12-month J-curve, not the 3-month default)** (J-curve duration alone is a ~$9.9M swing in DORA's example) ([Faros — DORA stress-test](https://www.faros.ai/blog/dora-ai-roi-calculator-telemetry-inputs)) · instrument **rework rate** (% specs re-touched within 7–21 days) as the PM-quality stand-in · documented **Go/No-Go** · package as a **private team plugin marketplace** (org git repo + `marketplace.json`, pinned in project `.claude/settings.json` so it spreads via version control) ([Claude Code — plugins/marketplaces](https://code.claude.com/docs/en/discover-plugins)) · teach the **untrusted-skill/prompt-injection rule** (plugins run at user privilege; install only from the org's trusted marketplace) · build + test a **kill switch** (disable each agent's write/publish scope instantly) · **train-the-trainer**: pilot champion becomes cohort-1 trainer, co-develop the prompt library *with* cohorts not for them ([AMD — champion enablement](https://www.amd.com/en/blogs/2025/transforming-amd-with-ai.html)) _(self-reported program design; the champion-as-trainer specific is our extrapolation)_ · hand over a **governance runbook + RACI** (named data-class owner, named approvers per agent, audit-log location, kill-switch + rotation playbook); implement "AI never publishes externally without a named approver" as a delegation chain with audit logs (aligns with EU AI Act Art. 14 human-oversight practice) · hand over the **eval contract** (golden set, rubric, CI workflow, thresholds, "how to add a case" runbook, nightly drift alerts) + a **2–4-week recurring error-analysis cadence on fresh traces** · **sell it honestly:** "instruments the baseline + leading indicators now, with a scheduled quarterly outcome read" — schedule the real comparison ~4 months out, before renewal · set a **30/60/90-day post-exit re-measure of adoption** (weekly-active-use among pilot PMs) + a **re-intervention SLA** (half-day champion refresh + an error-analysis pass) that fires if usage falls below the W6 exit bar — the measured win counts as **durable only once re-confirmed on the team's own, post-pod**.
- **Exit:** metric delta computed + ROI as a stress-tested range; documented Go/No-Go; installable plugin self-installs; kill switches + governance runbook handed over; champion ready to train cohort 1; eval contract + quarterly measurement plan scheduled; **30/60/90-day post-exit re-measure + re-intervention SLA scheduled**; expectations reset to leading-indicators-now / outcomes-later.
- **Watch-outs:** knowledge walks out → decision-capture discipline + train-the-trainer + expansion playbook · pilot purgatory → ship an *integrated, learning* system (MIT: purchased/partner builds succeed ~67% of the time vs internal "one-third as often") · ghost agents → governed sunset path · optimistic single ROI figure → stress-tested range.

**Enablement cohorts (W6+).** Launch each with a hackathon + the pilot team demoing real (messy) wins; **plan the ~60% skepticism wall** — HubSpot stalled past 60% and pushed to 90% with quantitative proof (incident-vs-adoption scatter), peer-validation videos, curated default config, and fluency in job descriptions — **beat skeptics with proof + peers, never mandates** ([HubSpot](https://product.hubspot.com/blog/context-is-key-how-hubspot-scaled-ai-adoption)); embed a 5-minute usage-win slot into an existing weekly call (don't add meetings); run quarterly portfolio reviews to retire underperformers and re-check EU AI Act risk tiers against the **2 Dec 2027** high-risk deadline (postponed from Aug 2026 by the November 2025 Digital Omnibus on AI; provisional agreement May 2026). **Cohorts inherit the rigor, not just the adoption mechanics:** each cohort gets the W6 eval *contract* (golden set, rubric, CI workflow, thresholds, "how to add a case" runbook) and runs a scoped error-analysis pass (A1.1 step 2) to seed its own golden set before building; **no cohort skill ships without an eval** (same no-eval-no-ship gate as W5, owned by the cohort champion). **Cohort cadence:** W1 reuse context+skills from the marketplace (no rebuild) · W2 error-analysis + golden set · W3–4 build + eval · W5 measure + handover; **exit = no-eval-no-ship cleared + cohort adoption past its own 60% wall.**

**Realistic time allocation:** participating PMs ~20–40% of the week during the active pod, ~10–20% in cohort phase; product owner heaviest; champions ~2–3 hrs/week; embed into existing sprint cycles, not a special project. ([StackAI — 30-day pilot](https://www.stackai.com/insights/how-to-pilot-enterprise-ai-in-30-days-the-fast-track-implementation-guide))
